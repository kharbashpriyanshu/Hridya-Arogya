What is Diabetes and Why Does it Hurt the Heart?

Diabetes is a condition that happens when your blood sugar (glucose) is too high. It develops when your pancreas doesn’t make enough insulin or any at all, or when your body isn’t responding to the effects of insulin properly.

Edited by-Patel Hirva

What happens in Diabetes?

When we eat, food turns into glucose (sugar) for energy. Insulin, a hormone made by the pancreas, helps move glucose from the blood into cells. In Diabetes, either:

  • The body doesn’t make enough insulin, or
  • The body doesn’t use it well (insulin resistance).

This leaves too much sugar in the blood. Diabetes is not just about sugar—it’s about how your body uses energy. When diabetes is uncontrolled, it silently damages blood vessels and nerves. That’s why people with diabetes are much more likely to develop heart problems.

How Diabetes Hurts the Heart

High blood sugar over time:
  • Damages blood vessels: makes them hard and narrow.
  • Raises bad cholesterol & lowers good cholesterol: faster plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Increases blood pressure: faster plaque buildup in arteries.
  • Damages nerves: people may not feel chest pain (silent heart attacks).

Diabetes can double or even triple the risk of heart disease and stroke.

Warning Signs to Watch

  • Fatigue, thirst, frequent urination.
  • Tingling in feet/hands.
  • Chest discomfort or shortness of breath (sometimes mild in diabetics).

Protecting Your Heart with Diabetes

  • Check blood sugar regularly and follow treatment.
  • Stay active—exercise improves insulin use and heart health.
  • Eat wisely—limit sugary foods, refined carbs, and trans fats.
  • Monitor BP and cholesterol—because together with diabetes, they multiply risk.
  • Quit smoking—it worsens artery damage.

Keeping BP in the Safe Zone

  • Normal: around 120/80 mm Hg
  • High: 130/80 or above
  • Low: below 90/60

Final Thought

Think of diabetes as sugar corroding the pipes of your body. Over time, the “pipes” (arteries) harden and clog, making the heart pump harder. Managing sugar doesn’t just protect you from diabetes complications—it saves your heart too.
